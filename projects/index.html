Maze Solver


with Jack Neus and Abiram Sritharan

Created at HackRU (Spring 2014)


Inspired by one of our Computer Science teachers at our school, we decided to create a Maze generator and solver with Java. Maze Solver has three key features.

1. Maze Generation

The user is first prompted for the height, width, and cell size, and a maze is then created using the Union Find algoithm. The Union Find algoithm generates a maze with all walls intact. The program randomly selects a cell and deletes one of the walls. This ensures that the maze is connected.

2. Human Solver (Yellow Line)

The Maze is first solved in the same manner as an intelligent human by keeping a "hand" on the left wall at all times.

3. Computer Solver (Red Line)

The maze is then solved in shortest distance possible.

BarBot








with Shreya Hirday, Shaantam Chawla, and Nishanth Shanmugham

Created at PennApps (Winter 2015)


BarBot utilizes a combination of Android and Arduino to create a magical Box that stores your temptations and keeps you from simply taking TOO MUCH. Well, it first started off as an idea for a robot that could move around and carry drinks for people at a party. Each person gets a limited numbers of drinks, and once that limit is reached...BarBot doesn't let you take anymore! We later expanded this idea to eating healthy as well. One of our favorite key features is that BarBot can automatically lift its cover so you can take what you went simply using sonar data. Another key feature that we like is that the Android app keeps track of how much you have had...and no registration required!
